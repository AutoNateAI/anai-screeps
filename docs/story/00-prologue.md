# Prologue: Touchdown

Before the spawn. Before the first harvester. Before any of it.

There was a drop pod, a countdown, and a callsign stenciled on the hull: AUTONATE-01. Somebody in Foundry branding apparently felt "Nate" needed a version bump before it shipped.

## The Foundry

The Foundry doesn't train architects in classrooms. No slide decks, no icebreaker where everyone says their favorite programming language, no "let's take this offline." They drop you into an unclaimed sector of the Screeps frontier with a terminal, a spawn beacon, and an AI collaborator riding in your headset, and they watch what you actually build — not what you said you'd build in the interview.

No map. No starter base. No teammates arguing about tabs versus spaces in a group chat. Just terrain, a controller, a couple of energy sources, and whatever code you're willing to write before the first tick runs out.

The rule the Foundry repeats to every architect before the hatch opens hasn't changed since the first cohort:

"The colony that survives isn't the one with the best pitch deck. It's the one whose systems keep running after everyone stops refreshing the dashboard."

Yes. They said "pitch deck." The Foundry has opinions about founders, and it's not wrong about most of them.

## Why You're Here

You applied. Honestly, you don't remember exactly when — maybe it was the fourth side project you shipped to an audience of nobody, maybe it was the friend who said, "you talk about systems the way other people talk about their fantasy football team." Either way, the Foundry doesn't recruit finished engineers. It recruits people who are already obsessed and just need a world that can keep up with them.

They gave you a callsign instead of a job title: AutoNate. Everyone who ships out gets one. Not everyone keeps it. The ones who quit go back to whatever they were doing before — probably somewhere with a much nicer PTO policy. The ones who build something that outlives their own attention span get to keep the name, and eventually hand it to the next person reckless enough to sign up.

## The Drop

The pod burns off atmosphere fast. By the time it's slow enough to matter, you're already down — hard landing, hatch venting, HUD rebooting one line at a time, like a laptop that's had forty Chrome tabs open since some incident in 2019 nobody talks about anymore.

Your AI collaborator comes online before you do.

"Telemetry's up," it says. "No hostiles in range. No structures on the ground. You've got sources, you've got a controller, and about as much energy sitting in this room as you'll ever get for free — which, if you've ever raised a pre-seed round, should sound extremely familiar. Everything after this, we build."

You climb out. Terrain in every direction. Two sources glowing faint green on the scan. A controller, unclaimed, waiting for the first structure that gives it a reason to matter.

No quest marker. No tutorial arrow. Just a blank room and a console with a blinking cursor — which, if you're being honest, is the most straightforward onboarding flow you've ever gotten.

Your collaborator is still talking. "First thing every Foundry architect learns the same way: pick a room, place a spawn, get one worker moving. Everything after that is downstream of this one decision. Sound familiar? Congratulations, you're building an MVP. It just happens to be made of sticks."

This is where Episode 1 picks up.
