# Prologue: Touchdown

Before the spawn. Before the first harvester. Before any of it.

There was a drop pod, a countdown, and a callsign stenciled on the hull: AUTONATE-01.

## The Foundry

The Foundry doesn't train architects in classrooms. It drops them into unclaimed sectors of the Screeps frontier with nothing but a terminal, a spawn beacon, and an AI collaborator wired into their headset, and it watches what they build.

No map. No starter base. No teammates on comms. Just terrain, a controller, a couple of energy sources, and the code you're willing to write before the first tick runs out.

The rule the Foundry repeats to every architect before the hatch opens is always the same one:

"The colony that survives isn't the one with the best plan. It's the one whose systems keep running after the plan stops mattering."

## Why You're Here

You applied. You don't remember exactly when — maybe it was the fourth failed side project, maybe it was the friend who said you talk about systems the way other people talk about sports. The Foundry doesn't recruit finished engineers. It recruits people who are already obsessed and just need a world worth being obsessed with.

They gave you a callsign instead of a job title: AutoNate. Everyone who ships out gets one. Not everyone keeps it. The ones who quit go back to being whoever they were before. The ones who build something that outlives their own attention span get to keep the name — and eventually, hand it to someone else.

## The Drop

The pod burns off atmosphere fast. By the time it's slow enough to matter, you're already down: hard landing, hatch venting, HUD rebooting one line at a time.

Your AI collaborator comes online before you do.

"Telemetry's up," it says. "No hostiles in range. No structures on the ground. You've got sources, you've got a controller, and about as much energy sitting in this room as you'll ever get for free. Everything after this, we build."

You climb out. Terrain in every direction. Two sources glowing faint green on the scan. A controller, unclaimed, waiting for the first structure that gives it a reason to matter.

No quest marker. No tutorial arrow. Just a blank room and a console with a blinking cursor.

Your collaborator is still talking. "First thing every Foundry architect learns the same way: pick a room, place a spawn, get one worker moving. Everything else is downstream of that."

This is where Episode 1 picks up.
